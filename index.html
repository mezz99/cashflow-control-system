The problem

Manual invoice tracking breaks down when follow-up is not visible.

Most B2B businesses already have the data. The issue is missing follow-up control: what is unpaid, what needs action today, and what has already been done.

Risk

Unpaid invoices sit too long

Invoices stay inside Excel or Google Sheets, but nobody receives a clear reminder when action is needed.

Visibility

Owners have to ask for updates

Managers often need to chase the admin for status, overdue amounts, and client payment risks.

Control

Follow-up history is scattered

Calls, emails, promises, disputes, and next reminder dates are easy to lose when there is no clean log.

What the system does

A practical control layer for your existing invoice spreadsheet.

It supports the accountant or admin. It does not replace them. The goal is to make unpaid invoice follow-up consistent, visible, and easier to manage.

Validates invoice rows

Checks required fields before the process runs, so missing invoice numbers, client details, due dates, amounts, or statuses are caught early.

Warns the admin when data is missing

Sends a clear internal warning when a row is not ready, instead of letting incomplete data quietly break follow-up.

Sends internal follow-up reminders

Alerts the right person when unpaid, overdue, disputed, or problem invoices need attention.

Records actions in a Follow-up Log

Keeps a simple history of what happened, when it happened, and what action should happen next.

Updates reminder dates

Moves the next follow-up date forward after an action, so the team always knows what is due next.

Sends an owner cashflow report

Gives the owner a readable summary of unpaid exposure, overdue invoices, problem accounts, and current follow-up status.

How it works

Simple enough for teams already using spreadsheets.

The system works with your invoice spreadsheet and follows a clear automated flow for validation, reminders, logs, and owner reporting.
